Work on Applied Plasma & Neutron Systems

Join a small team in Gandhinagar — approximately 5 km from IPR and ITER-India — working on accelerator-based BNCT, Gas Dynamic Trap medical-isotope production, and the long-horizon directions that follow. Incubated at AIC Plasmatech.

Why ASPL Fusion?

Work that matters

Your work contributes directly to BNCT cancer therapy — a targeted treatment designed to kill tumour cells while sparing healthy tissue — and to domestic medical-isotope capacity that reaches Indian hospitals.

Domestic capability

India is significantly import-dependent for medical isotopes. We are working on domestic production capability for Tc-99m, Lu-177, Cu-64, and other critical isotopes — improving resilience of the healthcare system.

Small team, long horizon

You join a small founding team at an early stage of a long-horizon programme, with room to take meaningful responsibility. The programme spans medical and industrial applications now; fusion-class applications sit at the far horizon.

Real Science

Publish papers, present at conferences, and work on problems across nuclear physics, plasma physics, and medical technology. Co-authorship on published work is the default.
