A Strategic Niche in the Global Fusion Race

The global pursuit of fusion is a monumental endeavor, dominated by large-scale, government-funded projects like the international ITER tokamak. These massive efforts are crucial for foundational science but often have multi-decade timelines. ASPL Fusion's strategy is different. By focusing on the architecturally simpler Gas Dynamic Trap (GDT)—a successful evolution of magnetic mirror concepts pioneered at labs like LLNL—we are charting a nimble, commercially-focused path. We aim to master neutron production for near-term industrial and medical markets, building a staircase of technological and commercial milestones towards a cost-effective fusion power plant. This approach allows us to innovate in parallel with, not in competition against, the mainstream efforts, positioning us to deliver value faster.

Phase 1: Foundation on Real-World Solutions

We begin by deploying a commercially standard D-T Accelerator to produce medical radioisotopes, specifically Molybdenum-99 (99Mo). This addresses a critical, undersupplied market in India, establishes our operational capabilities, and generates early revenue to fuel further innovation. Our target is to achieve a reliable output of **250 Curies (Ci) per week of Technetium-99m (⁹⁹ᵐTc)** via the clean ⁹⁸Mo(n,γ)⁹⁹Mo activation pathway, which generates only manageable Low-Level Waste (LLW).

Phase 2: The GDT Leap for Industrial Scale

This phase marks our technological leap to a volumetric neutron source using a Gas Dynamic Trap (GDT). This powerful device, requiring a D-T fusion power of approximately **1.0 MW** to generate a neutron flux of **~3.4x10¹⁴ n/s**, will drastically scale up isotope production. It also enables high-value services for the nuclear industry, including breeding fissile fuel from Thorium and transmuting long-lived nuclear waste, all within a structure built from advanced Reduced-Activation Ferritic-Martensitic (RAFM) steel.

The GDT Advantage: Simpler, More Stable, More Powerful

Unlike complex toroidal designs, the GDT's linear architecture is easier to build and scale. Its plasma behaves like a predictable fluid, making it inherently stable and an exceptionally effective source of high-energy neutrons—the engine for our industrial applications.

Phase 2.5: The Fusion-Fission Hybrid Bridge

We will leverage the GDT's intense neutron flux to drive a subcritical fission blanket. This hybrid approach multiplies energy output by over 10x, provides a pathway to burn nuclear waste, and enhances fuel sustainability for India's nuclear program—all with an inherent safety profile, as the fission process cannot sustain itself without the fusion core.

Phase 3: Delivering Limitless Power for India

Our ultimate vision is to scale the GDT technology into a standalone fusion power plant. By achieving net energy gain (Qfusion > 1), we will deliver clean, carbon-free, 24/7 baseload power, providing the backbone for a renewable-heavy grid and securing India's energy independence for generations to come.

🧬 Enhancing India's Healthcare Sovereignty

By establishing a reliable, domestic, reactor-free supply of critical medical isotopes, we insulate India's healthcare system from fragile global supply chains and empower doctors with the tools they need for life-saving diagnostics and therapies. Our initial production target is **250 Ci/week of ⁹⁹ᵐTc**, a significant contribution to meeting national demand.

Product Isotope Primary Use Market Significance
Tc-99m (from Mo-99) The workhorse of nuclear medicine, used in ~80% of diagnostic scans. Extremely high demand (~40M doses/year globally) with a vulnerable supply chain.
Lu-177 / Y-90 Targeted radionuclide therapy for treating cancers like prostate and liver. Rapidly expanding, high-value market for advanced cancer treatments.
