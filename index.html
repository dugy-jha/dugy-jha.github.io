Our Phased Pathway to Clean Energy

Our philosophy mitigates risk through early revenue generation, building a foundation of expertise on a logical path to commercial fusion energy.

Phase 1: Foundation on Real-World Solutions

Deploy a commercially standard D-T Accelerator Neutron Source to produce medical radioisotopes (99Mo), addressing a critical gap in India's domestic supply chain and generating early revenue.

Phase 2: The GDT Leap for Industrial Scale

Develop a volumetric neutron source using a Gas Dynamic Trap (GDT). This scales up isotope production, enables breeding of Uranium-233 from Thorium, and allows transmutation of long-lived nuclear waste.

The GDT Advantage: Simpler, Stable, Scalable

Its linear design is easier to engineer and maintain than toroidal devices, and the plasma is inherently more stable, providing an ideal and efficient neutron source.

Phase 2.5: Bridge to Commercial Energy

Leverage the GDT as a neutron source to drive a subcritical fission blanket. This hybrid approach multiplies energy output, breeds fuel, transmutes waste, and is inherently safe as the fission process cannot sustain itself without the fusion core.

Phase 3: Delivering Limitless Power for India

Our long-term vision: scaling the GDT technology to a full power plant, achieving net energy gain (Qfusion > 1). This will provide clean, carbon-free, 24/7 baseload power to complement renewables and secure India's energy independence.
