Our Phased Pathway to Clean Energy

Our philosophy mitigates risk through early revenue generation, building a foundation of expertise on a logical path to commercial fusion energy.

Phase 1: Foundation on Real-World Solutions

Deploy a commercially standard D-T Accelerator Neutron Source to produce medical radioisotopes (99Mo), addressing a critical gap in India's domestic supply chain. This initial phase targets India's ~$10 million annual market for Molybdenum-99, aiming to achieve profitability in under two years while establishing a secure domestic supply for hospitals and patients. [1, 1]

Phase 2: The GDT Leap for Industrial Scale

Develop a volumetric neutron source using a Gas Dynamic Trap (GDT). This powerful neutron source enables strategic applications like breeding fuel for the nuclear industry and transmuting long-lived radioactive waste, positioning ASPL to capture a significant share of the $1B+ global isotope market and transforming India from an importer to a key exporter. [1, 1]

The GDT Advantage: Simpler, Stable, Scalable

Its linear design is easier to engineer and maintain than toroidal devices, and the plasma is inherently more stable, providing an ideal and efficient neutron source.

Phase 2.5: Bridge to Commercial Energy

Leverage the GDT as a neutron source to drive a subcritical fission blanket. This hybrid approach multiplies energy output, breeds fuel, transmutes waste, and is inherently safe as the fission process cannot sustain itself without the fusion core.

Phase 3: Delivering Limitless Power for India

Our long-term vision: scaling the GDT technology to a full power plant, achieving net energy gain (Qfusion > 1). This plant will be the first of its kind (FOAK) in the nation, a 400 MW commercial facility designed to demonstrate utility-scale viability and provide clean, carbon-free, 24/7 baseload power to secure India's energy independence. [1, 1]
