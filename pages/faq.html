General Questions

What is fusion energy?

Fusion is the process that powers the sun. It involves combining light atomic nuclei to form a heavier nucleus, releasing an immense amount of energy. Unlike nuclear fission, it produces no long-lived radioactive waste, and its primary fuel, derived from water, is virtually limitless and domestically available.

Why is fusion energy important for India?

India's energy demand is projected to grow faster than any other nation. To meet this demand while fulfilling our decarbonization commitments, we need a clean, safe, and reliable source of 24/7 baseload power. Fusion is the ultimate solution to achieve energy sovereignty and power our economic growth sustainably.

Technology

What is an Advanced Magnetic Mirror and how is it different from a tokamak?

A tokamak uses a complex toroidal (donut-shaped) magnetic field to confine plasma. Our Advanced Magnetic Mirror uses a linear, inherently more stable magnetic field. This linear architecture is simpler to build, maintain, and scale, making it a more pragmatic and economically viable choice for a commercial power plant.

Is this technology safe?

Yes. Fusion is inherently safe. The conditions required for a fusion reaction are incredibly difficult to sustain, so there is no risk of a runaway chain reaction or meltdown. If the system fails, the plasma cools in seconds and the reaction stops. Furthermore, it produces no long-lived radioactive waste.

What makes your technology viable now?

Recent breakthroughs in High-Temperature Superconducting (HTS) magnets are a game-changer. These magnets allow us to create incredibly strong magnetic fields of over 25 Tesla, which dramatically improves plasma confinement and performance in our linear system, making it a highly efficient and powerful architecture for fusion.

Business & Investment

Why are you producing medical isotopes first?

Our phased strategy is our key differentiator. India is heavily import-dependent on critical medical isotopes. By entering this profitable market first (Phase 1), we address a national vulnerability and achieve profitability in under two years. This revenue funds our core fusion R&D, de-risking the entire venture for our investors.

What is your timeline to putting electricity on the grid?

Our Phase 2 device includes a sub-critical fusion-fission hybrid system designed to generate 50-100 MW of electricity as an early-revenue platform. Our first pure-fusion prototype power plant (Phase 3) is planned to be operational in years 11-15 of our roadmap, delivering approximately 400 MW of net electrical power to the grid.

How much funding are you seeking?

We are currently seeking $20 Million in Series A funding. These funds will be used to build our Phase 1 medical isotope facility, achieve profitability, and fund the initial R&D and engineering design for our Phase 2 Advanced Magnetic Mirror device.
