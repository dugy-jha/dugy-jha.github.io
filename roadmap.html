Our Roadmap

A strategic four-phase journey to commercial fusion energy

A Disciplined, Market-Driven Approach

ASPL Fusion has developed a pragmatic roadmap that balances technological ambition with commercial viability. Our four-phase strategy is designed to generate value at each stage while systematically de-risking our path to grid-scale fusion power.

This approach allows us to build a sustainable business model, establish operational expertise, and develop supply chains while progressively advancing our core technology.

Phase 1

Medical Isotope Production

2025-2028

Our initial focus is on developing fusion-based neutron sources for producing critical medical isotopes, particularly Molybdenum-99 (Mo-99), the precursor to Technetium-99m used in over 40 million medical procedures annually worldwide.

India currently imports 100% of its medical isotopes, creating vulnerabilities in the healthcare system. Our technology will establish domestic production capability, ensuring a stable supply for cancer diagnostics and treatment.

Key Milestones

  • 2025 Complete proof-of-concept prototype
  • 2026 Achieve first neutron production
  • 2027 Demonstrate Mo-99 production
  • 2028 Secure regulatory approvals
  • 2028 Begin commercial isotope sales

Technology Validation

Demonstrate our magnetic mirror fusion concept in a commercial application

Initial Revenue

Establish sustainable business model with recurring revenue stream

Strategic Value

Secure India's medical isotope supply chain for critical healthcare needs

Phase 2

Industrial Heat Applications

2028-2031

Building on our success with medical isotopes, we will scale our technology to provide clean process heat for industrial applications. Hard-to-abate sectors like steel, cement, and chemicals require high-temperature heat that is difficult to decarbonize with conventional renewables.

Our fusion systems will deliver temperatures up to 800Â°C, meeting the needs of many industrial processes while eliminating carbon emissions and reducing dependency on imported fossil fuels.

Key Milestones

  • 2028 Enhanced neutron output system
  • 2029 Heat exchange system integration
  • 2030 Pilot industrial deployment
  • 2031 First commercial heat contracts

Expanded Revenue

Larger market opportunity with higher-value contracts

Carbon Reduction

Significant emissions savings in hard-to-decarbonize sectors

Energy Independence

Reduced reliance on imported natural gas for industrial processes

Phase 3

Hydrogen Production

2031-2034

As our technology continues to advance, we will apply it to green hydrogen production. Using the high-temperature heat from our fusion systems for high-temperature electrolysis dramatically improves efficiency compared to conventional electrolysis methods.

This clean hydrogen will serve as both an energy carrier and feedstock for industries like fertilizer production, refineries, and heavy transportation â all critical sectors for India's economy.

Key Milestones

  • 2031 High-temperature electrolysis integration
  • 2032 Demonstration hydrogen production facility
  • 2033 Industrial-scale hydrogen production
  • 2034 Commercial hydrogen supply contracts

Energy Storage

Enable long-duration energy storage through hydrogen

Fertilizer Security

Support domestic fertilizer production without natural gas imports

Transport Decarbonization

Enable zero-emission heavy transportation and shipping

Phase 4

Grid-Scale Fusion Power

2034-2040

The culmination of our roadmap is grid-scale fusion power generation. By this stage, we will have developed extensive operational experience, supply chains, and technical capabilities through our earlier phases, significantly de-risking this final step.

Our first power plants will be designed for India's specific grid requirements, providing clean, reliable baseload electricity to complement variable renewable generation and support the country's growing energy needs.

Key Milestones

  • 2034 Prototype power conversion system
  • 2036 First grid-connected fusion demonstrator
  • 2038 Commercial-scale power plant design
  • 2040 First commercial power plant operational

Energy Security

Unlimited clean energy with domestic resources

Grid Stability

24/7 reliable power to complement variable renewables

Economic Growth

Enable India's continued economic expansion with abundant clean energy
